Man behind the legend


With rain falling and the mercury hovering just above freezing, he pinned the throttle, dropped the clutch and performed a monstrous burnout in the middle of the road.
Satisfied the Midlands were suitably sluiced in tire smoke, he gunned his heavily modified, 1,003bhp, Japanese-registered, gold-painted Toyota Mk.4 Supra with one aim in mind: hitting 200mph.

Smoky nagat's supra


As the company's youngest worker, founder Kazuhiko "Smokey" Nagata started out as a mechanic for Toyota. At Trust/GReddy, he later worked as a tuner and fabricator. In 1978, he started using the Trust shop for personal projects, and as a nickname for his covert work at the Trust facility, "Top Secret" was born. Because Nagata was a highly skilled worker and a valued asset to the company, his employers permitted this to continue.[2][3] In 1991, Top Secret broke away from Trust and opened their own store in Chiba. The business started showing at the Tokyo Auto Salon in 1993.[4]
