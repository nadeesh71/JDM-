Man behind the legend



On November 4, 1998, at around four in the morning,
Kazuhiko "Smokey" Nagata shifted from the hard shoulder into lane two on the A1(M) and stopped.
As the rain started to fall and the temperature was just above freezing,
he let go of the clutch, pinned the throttle, and executed an incredible burnout in the middle of the road. He drove his massively modified, 1,003 horsepower, Japanese-registered, gold-painted Toyota Mk.4 Supra with the intention of reaching 200 mph, content that the Midlands had been sufficiently covered in tire smoke.
