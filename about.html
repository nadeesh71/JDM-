1. lexus LFA


The Lexus LFA is powered by a 72-degree bank angle 4,805 cc (4.8 L; 293.2 cu in)
V10 engine equipped with Dual VVT-i carrying the 1LR-GUE designation with a maximum output of 412 kW (560 PS; 553 hp) delivered at 8,700 rpm.
Its maximum torque output of 480 N⋅m (354 lb⋅ft) arrives at 6,800 rpm,
90 percent of which is available from 3,700 rpm.



2. Toyota 2000 GT


The Toyota 2000GT is a limited-production front mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive, two-door, two-seat sports car/grand tourer designed by Toyota in collaboration with Yamaha.
First displayed to the public at the Tokyo Motor Show in 1965,
the 2000GT was manufactured under contract by Yamaha between 1967 and 1970.



3. 2017 Acura NSX


The NSX was designed by a team led by Chief Designer Masahito Nakano and Executive Chief Engineer Shigeru Uehara. It benefited from advanced aerodynamics and styling inspired by the F-16 fighter jet cockpit as well as input, during the final development stages, from Formula One World Champion Ayrton Senna.

4. 2020 Toyota GR supra




Produced by Toyota since 2019, the GR Supra (model code J29/DB or A90/A91 for marketing purposes) is a sports automobile.
\ The Toyota Gazoo Racing (TGR) brand worked with BMW to develop and market the GR Supra, the fifth generation of the Supra. It is the A80 Supra's replacement, after the latter's manufacture was discontinued in 2002.
With a short wheelbase, wide track, and low center of gravity,
the revolutionary sports vehicle platform that Toyota and BMW jointly developed is the basis for the GR Supra and the G29 BMW Z4.[10][11] Tada-san, the top engineer of BMW, first suggested that the new Supra be built on a pre-existing platform of their own.[12] The Magna Steyr facility located in Graz, Austria, produces both automobiles.[13]


4.Skyline r34





1998 saw the release of the Skyline R34, the tenth generation.
In order to maximize motion performance and maintain adequate internal space in a smaller body, the body was quite stiff. Released in 1999, the "GT-R" model is its highest performance model. The "V⋅spec" garnered a lot of attention because of its race car-inspired appearance, specially tuned springs, active LSD, and distinctive aero elements that improved traveling performance. The 2000 addition of V-spec II featured aluminum pedals and a carbon engine hood with *NACA duct,
creating a Spartan environment. This vehicle is a special edition V-spec II in the color "Bayside Blue."
